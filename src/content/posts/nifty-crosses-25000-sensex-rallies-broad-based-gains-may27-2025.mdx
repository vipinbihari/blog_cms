# Nifty Reclaims 25,000, Sensex Soars as Bulls Dominate Dalal Street

Indian equity markets continued their upward trajectory for a second consecutive session on Monday, May 26, 2025, with the NSE Nifty 50 decisively reclaiming the crucial 25,000 mark and the BSE Sensex closing robustly above 82,000, driven by widespread buying interest, particularly in auto and IT stocks, and a confluence of positive domestic and global factors.

## Market Roars: Key Numbers and Movers

The trading session on May 26, 2025, saw Dalal Street buzzing with activity. The **NSE Nifty 50** index climbed 148 points, or 0.60%, to settle at **25,001.15**. During the day, the Nifty 50 even touched an intraday high of 25,079.20.

Similarly, the **BSE Sensex**, the 30-share benchmark index, jumped 455.37 points, or 0.56%, to close at **82,176.45**. The Sensex had an even stronger intraday performance, surging 771.16 points or 0.94% to hit 82,492.24 at one point. This marked the Sensex's highest closing level in over a week.

The market breadth was positive, indicating broad-based participation in the rally. On the NSE, approximately 1,735 shares advanced, while 1,173 shares declined, and 74 shares remained unchanged. Some reports indicated even higher numbers of advancing stocks on the BSE, with 2,291 advances against 1,747 declines. The overall market capitalisation of NSE-listed companies stood at $5.18 trillion at the close.

Volatility, as measured by the India VIX, edged higher, jumping 4.28% to 18.02, suggesting that while the sentiment was bullish, some caution persisted among traders.

## Sectoral Strength and Stock-Specific Action

The rally was largely led by strong performances in the **auto, IT, and metal sectors**.

Top Nifty gainers included Bajaj Auto (up 2.39%), JSW Steel (+2.13%), Mahindra & Mahindra (M&M) (+2.07%), Trent, and Hindalco. M&M was also a top Sensex gainer, rising 2.2%. Other significant gainers on the Sensex included HCL Technologies, Tata Motors, Nestle India, ITC, Hindustan Unilever, Larsen & Toubro, and Tech Mahindra.

Divi's Labs also saw a notable jump of 3.5% on the Nifty after announcing a global pharma supply deal. JK Cement surged 6% following reports of strong volume growth.

On the flip side, some stocks faced selling pressure. Eternal (formerly Zomato) was a prominent laggard, falling around 4.5% after FTSE and MSCI reduced the stock's weightage in their indices. Other losers included UltraTech Cement, Kotak Mahindra Bank, Power Grid, and NTPC.

Mid-cap and small-cap indices also participated in the rally, with the Nifty Smallcap100 and Nifty Midcap100 indices settling higher by 0.67% and 0.37%, respectively.

## Catalysts Behind the Bull Run

Several factors contributed to the positive market sentiment:

1.  **RBI's Record Dividend:** The Reserve Bank of India's announcement of a record ₹2.69 lakh crore dividend to the government for FY25 significantly boosted investor confidence. Analysts believe this substantial transfer will aid the government in fiscal consolidation and potentially support lower interest rates. V K Vijayakumar of Geojit Financial noted that this dividend could help contain the fiscal deficit target for FY26 at 4.4%.
2.  **Positive Global Cues:** Easing global trade tensions played a role. Reports indicated that US President Donald Trump postponed recently proposed 50% tariffs on the European Union to July 9, which was received positively by markets. Strong cues from European markets, with the DAX and STOXX 50 trading higher, and bullish U.S. market futures also supported the sentiment.
3.  **Domestic Macro Factors:** The early arrival of the monsoon in parts of India has raised hopes for a good agricultural season and improved rural demand, further buoying market spirits. Additionally, optimism surrounding India's economic outlook, with NITI Aayog CEO BVR Subrahmanyam stating that India has become the fourth largest economy in the world, overtaking Japan, added to the positive near-term morale.
4.  **Rupee Strength:** The Indian Rupee strengthened sharply against the US dollar, opening at 85.02 and jumping by up to 40 paise – its strongest single-day rise since November 2022. This was attributed to a softer dollar index and the RBI's dividend transfer.
5.  **Institutional Buying:** Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs) turned net purchasers, buying ₹135 crore worth of stocks on May 26th. Domestic Institutional Investors (DIIs) were also strong net buyers, with purchases worth ₹1,745 crore, indicating robust domestic confidence.

## Expert Commentary

Market experts largely view the current momentum positively, though with an eye on key resistance levels.

Nandish Shah, Senior Derivative & Technical Research Analyst at HDFC Securities, commented that the Nifty seems to have regained its momentum, breaking out from its consolidation zone of 24,500-25,000. He identified immediate resistance for Nifty at 25,207 (derived from Fibonacci retracement) and support at 24,800.

Ajit Mishra, SVP, Research at Religare Broking, highlighted that it will be crucial for Nifty to surpass the resistance hurdle of 25,200 to regain upward momentum. He advised traders to remain stock-specific and use dips as buying opportunities in quality names.

Praveen Dwarakanath, Vice President of Hedged.in, noted that Nifty's momentum on the upside in higher timeframes indicates a possible breakout towards 25,700 levels.

Om Mehra, Technical Research Analyst at SAMCO Securities, pointed out that the Nifty daily RSI remains stable at 61, suggesting healthy momentum without being overbought, and that the index is comfortably above short-term moving averages.

## What to Watch Next

As the market enters trading on May 27, 2025, several factors and levels will be keenly watched:

*   **Nifty Technical Levels:** Immediate resistance for Nifty is seen around the 25,200 - 25,250 zone. A sustained move above this could propel the index towards 25,300 and potentially 25,650 - 25,700. On the downside, immediate support is pegged near 24,800 - 24,900, with more significant support in the 24,400 - 24,500 region (coinciding with the 20-Day Exponential Moving Average).
*   **Bank Nifty:** The Bank Nifty, which closed at 55,571 (up 0.31%), faces resistance near 55,800 - 56,100, with key support levels between 55,300 and 54,800.
*   **Global Cues:** Developments in global markets, particularly US inflation data and central bank commentary, will continue to influence sentiment.
*   **F&O Expiry Dynamics:** While not an immediate market driver for May 26th's close, SEBI's recent circular mandating that equity derivatives contract expiries for all exchanges happen either on Tuesday or Thursday (with exchanges to submit proposals by June 15) will be a key regulatory development to watch. This could alter trading patterns and market share dynamics between NSE and BSE in the medium term.
*   **Corporate Actions:** Several companies are going ex-dividend this week, including ManInfraconstruction, L&T Finance, and Trident on May 27th, followed by Colgate Palmolive and ITC on May 28th, and Infosys and Bajaj Finance on May 30th. Ujaas Energy is also set for a bonus issue (17:25) with a record date of May 30th.

The current market rally has brought cheer to investors, but it's essential to remain watchful of global developments and domestic triggers. For retail investors, maintaining a diversified portfolio and focusing on fundamentally strong companies remains a prudent approach.
